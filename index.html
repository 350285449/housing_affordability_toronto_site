Toronto Housing pressure report

Housing costs are pushing people out.

Toronto estimated 15,418 people experiencing homelessness in 2024. In our 125-person community survey, housing affordability scored only 3.76 out of 10.

The clearest takeaway: when affordable homes are scarce, it becomes harder to keep a home and harder to leave a shelter.

01 / The answer

Start with the conclusion

More affordable housing gives people a better chance to stay housed.

Housing is not the only cause of homelessness. But without a home someone can afford, every other problem becomes harder to solve.

01

Pressure begins here

When housing takes too much income, there is less room for food, transit, savings, and emergencies.

This does not mean every rent-burdened household becomes homeless. It means more households are exposed when something goes wrong.

What this project can say

The city data and survey point in the same direction: housing pressure is severe and people support action. They show a strong pattern, not proof that housing cost is the only cause of homelessness.
